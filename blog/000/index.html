Code / Code / Code

My first blog post ever! My name is Sean and today is the day I start blogging my journey through The Iron Yard Academy. I am hoping that this will serve as a journal of my weekly thoughts, challenges, victories and progress. I hope this also is inspiring others to take action on their dreams. I have spent years wanting to drop everything and learn to code but always had an excuse/reason of why not. I have been itching dreaming of being a full time developer for a long time. Creating something is just one part of my inspiration to become a developer. Not many people wake up to a job they want or even in a field they want. I know that has been my journey in life thus far! I don’t want these blog posts to be incredibly long and I also don’t want to hit you with my life/career story all on entry one. So, we will get back to my story another time. For now what’s happening at TIYA?! Code immersion! It feels like somedays I get it and the next…

It’s a struggle EVERYDAY! I’m not saying this is bad at all. Coming into this I expected the most intense learning pace. I’ll say it again, code immersion! I rarely am doing anything besides something to do with code. Eat/sleep/code sounds hard and it sounds crazy… It’s also awesome, I really try to spend as much time as I can doing something to hone my skills. Week 1 and 2 are really a blur, I have learned tons. It’s been almost entirely Ruby up to this point. Week 2 project was a console blackjack game. It proved to be nearly all Friday, Saturday and Sunday to get it about 90% completed. Best effort is what the aim is for now. I am hoping to come back to it with later learn skills to be much more efficient. Eat/sleep/code isn’t really my story so far. What I’m doing it’s code/code/code. When I’m eating I am watching a tutorial, keynote or reading something! When I’m sleeping I’m dreaming about code. No really! Samantha, my wife, can attest to that. I have an 11 month old daughter, Kennedy, who rarely makes it through the night without needing something. So, last night Samantha asked me to get a diaper, TEAMWORK! Being in that just barely awake 1am frame of mind she tells me I asked her, “what card she needed?” Hilarious! Dreaming of code is real! I did get her the diaper but never found out about what card she wanted. ———————————————— You’re in! Nice. We’ve put together a little post to introduce you to the Ghost editor and get you started. Go ahead and edit this post to get going and learn how it all works!

Getting Started

Writing in markdown is really easy. In the left hand panel of Ghost, you simply write as you normally would. Where appropriate, you can use formatting shortcuts to style your content. For example, a list:

  • Item number one
  • Item number two
    • A nested item
  • A final item

or with numbers!

  1. Remember to buy some milk
  2. Drink the milk
  3. Tweet that I remembered to buy the milk, and drank it

Links

Want to link to a source? No problem. If you paste in url, like http://ghost.org - it’ll automatically be linked up. But if you want to customise your anchor text, you can do that too! Here’s a link to the Ghost website. Neat.

Working with Code

Got a streak of geek? We’ve got you covered there, too. You can write inline <code> blocks really easily with back ticks. Want to show off something more comprehensive? 4 spaces of indentation gets you there.

1
2
3
4
awesome-thing {
  display: block;
  width: 100%;
}

Ready for a Break?

Throw 3 or more dashes down on any new line and you’ve got yourself a fancy new divider. Aw yeah.

Advanced Usage

There’s one fantastic secret about Markdown. If you want, you can write plain old HTML and it’ll still work! Very flexible.
